About

The Sustain-A-Thon is a sustainability-themed design and build competition. Teams of 4 students will compete to design and prototype a solution to the design prompt. Prototyping materials, tools, and workspace will be provided.

FAQ

Who can participate?
Everyone! While members of the host organizations were given priority registration, the RSVP is now open to anybody!
Is there a prize for the winning team?
Yes! There is a $300 cash prize for the winning team.
Can I choose my team?
While we are strongly encouraging people to go with a randomized team in the spirit of community and networking, we are allowing people to request 1 partner. Partner requests must be mutual and will be submitted through a form sent out after you have RSVP’d. The other members of the team will be randomized.
I don’t have any solid technical experience, can I still contribute?
Yes! We will be hosting workshops and office hours throughout the workday that focus on different skills and how to use machinery around the Envision Lab. The workshops are a perfect introduction for beginners while the office hours will provide an additional resource for beginners and experienced participants alike.
Do I have to be there all 3 days?
The only mandatory times are as follows:

Friday: 6 pm – 8 pm (introduction, prompt reveal, speakers, team reveal)

Sunday: 12 pm – 4 pm (presentations, awards)

However, we do expect all participants to communicate openly with their teams and make an effort to contribute to the team. On Friday evening there will be time to talk about expectations and what times you all expect to be there working. All day Saturday, as well as Sunday morning, are work time for your team and participants can come and go as needed.
What if I don’t come up with any ideas for the prompt?
The prompt will be revealed on Friday evening. We will be having 4-5 speakers that are relevant to the prompt give short presentations on Friday to set the stage for some directions you could go with your project. Also, working in a team may bring up ideas you never considered.
What do I bring?
Prior to coming to the Sustain-a-thon, please ensure you have filled out the Student Responsibility Contract for the EnVision Maker Studio, which can be found here. You must also wear closed toed shoes, as well as tie long hair back and abstain from wearing loose or dangling clothing or jewelry. You may want to bring your laptop, a blanket, a charger, and extra snacks.
Will food be provided?
Yes—Friday dinner, Saturday breakfast/lunch/snacks, and Sunday breakfast/lunch will all be provided.
Is it free?
The cost of participation for this event is $20. Payment can be made to our Venmo (@ESWUCSD) or by cash to our VP of Finance, Tanner Hanson. To pay in cash, please contact eswucsdvpf@gmail.com. For Venmo payments, please title, "Sustainability Make-A-Thon - [your name]". This money goes toward materials, food, prizes, and more!
