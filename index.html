FAQ

What is Sustain-A-Thon?
Sustain-a-thon is a sustainability focused make-a-thon. The difference between a make-a-thon and a hack-a-thon is that a hack-a-thon focuses on the final product or idea, whereas a make-a-thon focuses on the process of creating and rapid prototyping. The goal of our Sustain-a-thon is to have you build devices or products that address the sustainability challenge at hand.
Where is it?
The Sustain-a-thon is located in the Structural and Materials Science Engineering Building at the third floor in the EnVision Maker Studio.
When is it?
This make-a-thon competition will be held on February 1, 2019 through February 3, 2019. The first day will be February 1st at 6:00 PM to 9:00 PM, day 2 is February 2nd from 9:00 AM to midnight, and day 3 is February 3rd at 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM.
Who can participate?
Only members of the host student organizations may attend this Sustain-a-thon. Furthermore, the competition is hosted in the EnVision space, which allows for participation of UCSD Engineering and Visual Arts majors only.
Do I need a team?
Teams will be assigned during the event.
Is it free?
If you are a registered member of one of the participating organizations, the cost of participation for this event is $20. Payment can be made to our Venmo (@ESWUCSD) or by cash to our VP of Finance, Tanner Hanson. To pay in cash, please contact eswucsdvpf@gmail.com. For Venmo payments, please title, "Sustainability Make-a-Thon - [your name]".
What do I bring?
Prior to coming to the Sustain-a-thon, please ensure you have filled out the Student Responsibility Contract for the EnVision Maker Studio, which can be found here. You must also wear closed toed shoes, as well as tie long hair back and abstain from wearing loose or dangling clothing or jewelry. You may want to bring your laptop, a blanket, and extra snacks.
Will food be provided?
Yes - dinner on Friday night, breakfast/lunch/snacks on Saturday, and breakfast/lunch on Sunday will be provided.
Is there a prize for the winning team?
Yes - there will be cash prizes for both the winning team and the runner-up team.
